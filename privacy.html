Privacy Statement

How the Dominion Chihuahua Club Inc collects, uses, stores, and protects your personal information, in accordance with the New Zealand Privacy Act 2020.

Last updated: June 2026

1. Who We Are

The Dominion Chihuahua Club Inc (“the Club”, “we”, “us”, “our”) is a registered incorporated society under the Incorporated Societies Act 2022, based in Canterbury, New Zealand. We are committed to protecting the privacy of our members, applicants, and anyone who interacts with us.

This Privacy Statement applies to all personal information collected by the Club, whether through our website, membership applications, email correspondence, or at Club events.

2. What Personal Information We Collect

We may collect the following personal information:

  • Full name
  • Postal and email address
  • Phone number
  • Dogs New Zealand registration details (where applicable)
  • Payment confirmation (we do not store card or bank account numbers)
  • Any other information you choose to provide in correspondence with us

We collect only information that is necessary for the purpose it is collected. We do not collect sensitive personal information (such as health or financial data) unless you choose to provide it voluntarily.

3. How We Collect Personal Information

We collect personal information directly from you when you:

  • Submit a membership application (online or in writing)
  • Contact us by email or through this website
  • Attend Club events, playgroups, or shows
  • Pay a membership fee or event entry fee

We do not collect personal information from third parties without your knowledge, except where permitted by law.

4. Why We Collect Personal Information

We collect and use personal information to:

  • Process and manage your Club membership
  • Communicate with you about Club events, playgroups, shows, and news
  • Comply with our obligations as a registered incorporated society
  • Maintain accurate membership and financial records
  • Respond to enquiries and requests for information

We will not use your personal information for any purpose other than the purpose for which it was collected, unless you have given consent or we are required to do so by law.

5. Who We Share Your Information With

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information. We may share your information only:

  • With elected Club committee members, for the purpose of administering your membership
  • With Dogs New Zealand where required for show entry or registration purposes
  • With third parties who provide services to the Club (such as payment processors), solely to the extent necessary to provide those services
  • Where we are legally required to do so

6. How We Store and Protect Your Information

Your personal information is held securely by the Club. We take reasonable steps to protect personal information from loss, unauthorised access, use, modification, or disclosure.

Information is stored in electronic form (email and digital records) accessible only to authorised committee members. Physical records, where used, are kept securely. We retain personal information only for as long as it is needed for the purpose it was collected, or as required by law.

7. Cookies and Website Analytics

Our website is a static site hosted on GitHub Pages. We do not use cookies for tracking, advertising, or profiling purposes. We do not operate any analytics services that collect personal information about website visitors.

8. Your Rights Under the Privacy Act 2020

Under the New Zealand Privacy Act 2020, you have the right to:

  • Access the personal information we hold about you
  • Correct any personal information that is inaccurate, incomplete, or out of date
  • Request deletion of personal information we hold about you, subject to any legal obligations to retain it
  • Complain to the Office of the Privacy Commissioner if you believe your privacy rights have been breached

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the details below. We will respond to access and correction requests within 20 working days, as required by the Act.

9. Privacy Complaints

If you have concerns about how we have handled your personal information, please contact us in the first instance so we can attempt to resolve the matter. If you are not satisfied with our response, you may contact the Office of the Privacy Commissioner:

  • Website: www.privacy.org.nz
  • Phone: 0800 803 909

10. Changes to This Statement

We may update this Privacy Statement from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or legal requirements. The current version will always be available on this page, with the date of the last update noted at the top.
